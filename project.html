Case Study
Cani Agribusiness Limited
Cashew has been described to be the next cocoa for Ghana. Its importance to the economy of the country cannot be over emphasized with tremendous output and revenue (over USD 900 million revenue in 2017 for Ghana) in recent years. According to research, there are vast hectares of land suitable for the cultivation of the crop in the Northern and Brong-Ahafo Regions but these lies uncultivated. This has created the need to improve production of cashew to improve the economy and alleviate poverty in the regions. Cani Agribusiness Limited was purposely set-up to take advantage of the huge potential in this growing cashew industry. It is a Ghanaian based company that undertakes farming and agricultural processing. The company is determined to introduce cutting edge technology into the Ghanaian cashew farming business, starting with plantation farming and branching into agro processing in the near future. Cani Agribusiness has, acquired 300 acres freehold land in Badu, located in the Tain District in the Brong-Ahafo Region to undertake cashew farm. IMIL has been engaged as the transaction advisors on this project.

Case Study
Candela Export and Import Limited (CANEXIM)
Trade amongst nations strengthen local currencies, creates jobs and ultimately create wealth. It is on this account that nations strive to increase trade among themselves. Several attempts have been made and are currently being considered to increase trade among nations around the globe with the latest attempt in Africa being the African Continental Free Trade Agreement, which is promulgated to ensure free trade among African countries. Whilst these efforts are currently under the negotiation phases, the challenges to foster free movement of goods from Ghana to other parts of the world still persist. Ghana’s export is segmented into traditional and non-traditional exports, where the traditional exports are reputed to be better managed as a result of effective regulatory oversight. The same, however, cannot be said of the Non-Traditional Exports (NTEs), despite its immense contribution to the economy. It is on this account that CANEXIM is undertaking this project to provide support to the NTEs sector. The project is designed to support and grow Ghana’s non-traditional sector to become a bigger player in Ghana’s export sector through the support of IMIL who have been contracted as the transaction advisors and consults. CANEXIM expect to properly position the NTEs sector through a well-tuned three-phased project intervention that is designed to make the company a player in the export industry as well as help Ghana reap maximum benefit from its export endeavours. The three phased intervention comprises rendering agency services, providing warehousing/online marketing and factoring, with these three interventions envisaged to be implemented without a one-year period.

Case Study
GUBA Heritage Shops
The Ghana Tourism Authority has made significant strides to improve tourism in the country with the creation of the National Tourism Development Plan (2013-2027). The reason for this significant effort in the tourism industry is to ensure that Ghanaian culture is proudly exposed to the international world and also to create employment whilst generating significant revenue for the country. Although these efforts are very important and effective in achieving the desired target, there are significant number of people who do not visit the country and have no intentions of visiting the country. It is important that this group of people are also exposed to the rich cultures of the country. GUBA is poised to reach this group with series of activities, events and other showcases. GUBA Social Enterprise, which consist of GUBA Awards, GUBA Expo, GUBA Foundation, GUBA Careers and GUBA Health, is an umbrella organization committed to the advancement of Ghana and its citizens. GUBA Social Enterprise, through its GUBA Expo, is aiming to advance the sale of Made in Ghana products in the United Kingdom through the establishment of Ghana-themed heritage shops at high end streets in London and other UK cities. Based on its existing experience in exposing Ghanaian culture in the United Kingdom, its team sees this venture as a critical step towards achieving its overall objective of exposing the riches of Ghana to the outside world. IMIL was accordingly contracted to lead the process towards securing working capital for the company. Its engagement entailed preparing a business plan and undertaking credit facilitation for the company to raise the needed funds for the project.
