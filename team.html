Our well-resourced team of professional researchers, consultants and business executives have solid global strategic alliances and network to deliver with speed and extra-ordinary customer care, crowning it with a guaranteed satisfaction to our key stakeholders.

Agyenna Kesse-Tachi

Chief Executive Officer

Agyenna is an experienced consultant and researcher with vast experience in business, strategic and marketing planning; M&E and feasibility study spanning seven years. He has successfully prepared tender documents, restructured businesses and helped recapitalize businesses.

Team Profile
Agyenna Kesse-Tachi - CEO
Agyenna is an experienced consultant and researcher with vast experience in business, strategic and marketing planning; M&E and feasibility study spanning seven years. He has successfully prepared tender documents, restructured businesses and helped recapitalize businesses. He was the lead consultant in developing a business plan for the construction of Range 2 Industries, Neuro Surgery Africa, Legon Golf Driving Range/Training Center and Point-Blank Media Concepts. He has undertaken several assignments for EDAIF (now Eximbank), including playing a crucial role that culminated in building Eskay Therapeutic Limited and Lymens Medical Supplies’ ultra-modern pharmaceutical plants. He was the lead consultant in restructuring Life and Leisure Stores Ltd in Nigeria, Geoquaye Company Limited and Edge S and S. Agyenna undertook feasibility study for Concrete Product Limited to establish a block factory and for the FAO’s National Forest Investment Program (NFIP) for the West Africa Forest Convergence Plan. He was the lead consultant in undertaking an end-line M&E for Penplusbyte among others. He is a development expert, social worker, statistician and an entrepreneur with an MA and MPhil Degrees from the Institute of Statistical Social and Economic Research (ISSER), University of Ghana, Legon and a BA (Social Work) from the same institution

Dr Bernard Nsiah

Agribusiness

Bernard is a consultant who has over ten (10) years experience setting-up Quality Management Systems and monitoring the performance of the systems for small, medium and large-scale farms.

Team Profile
Dr Bernard Nsiah - Agricultural Economist
Bernard is a consultant in Quality Systems Management and M&E. He has over ten (10) years’ experience setting-up Quality Management Systems and monitoring the performance of the systems for small, medium and large-scale farms, farmer-based organizations, agro-processors and exporters. He has set-up management systems and monitored the performance of these systems for various farms and companies in Ghana, Nigeria, Ivory Coast, Cameroon, Kenya, Tanzania, Zambia, Malawi, Germany, Austria and Croatia. Dr. Nsiah’s rich experience in global standards such as ISO 9001:2008, Organic Agriculture (European Union, National Organic Program [USA], Japanese Agricultural Standard), Global G.A.P. (Fruits and Vegetables), Roundtable for Sustainable Palm Oil (RSPO), Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points (HACCP), Sustainable Agriculture Network (SAN) standards, etc. enable him to monitor and evaluate the performance of farms, farmer-based organizations, agro-processors and exporters. Bernard holds PhD in Agricultural Economics and Masters in Natural Resoruces Management from the Technical University of Dresden, Germany and has been with Impact Initiatives since June 2016 as a Senior Consultant.

Dr. Richmond Atta-Ankomah

Economist and Statistics

Richmond has a research background in economics and industrial development issues, spanning several specific areas including manufacturing, health as well a M&E. His research work in the last five years has focused on manufacturing and extractive industries in Africa.

Team Profile
Dr. Richmond Atta-Ankomah - Economist and Statistician
Richmond is an economist and a Research Fellow at the Institute of Statistical Social and Economic Research (ISSER), University of Ghana and a consultant with Impact Initiatives Limited. He received his bachelor’s and master’s degrees in Economics from the University of Ghana in 2004 and 2007 respectively and a PhD from the Open University, UK in 2014. He has over 10 years of research/professional experience. Before joining ISSER in December 2016 and consulting with Impact Initiatives, he worked for the Open University of UK for about 2 years as a Research Fellow. Richmond has a research background in economics and industrial development issues, spanning several specific areas including manufacturing, health as well a M&E. Currently, Richmond’s research interests are focused on enterprise level analysis particularly from the perceptive of economics of innovation, (global) value chain analysis, and trade and investment issues in sub Saharan Africa. His research work in the last five years has focused on manufacturing and extractive industries in Africa. He has also worked on consultancy basis for several organisations such as UNDP (Ghana office), Oxfam Ghana and JMK consulting group.
